# LinkedIn Guide — NaviOpsEnterprise

How to turn the work you do here into a LinkedIn presence that gets recruiters to message you for
IT-support roles. Pair with [`PORTFOLIO-GUIDE.md`](PORTFOLIO-GUIDE.md) and each lesson's §10.

## The principle
Recruiters search LinkedIn by keyword and skim headlines. Your job: make the keywords true, the
headline clear, and the proof one click away (your GitHub repo).

## Headline
Lead with the role you're targeting + your edge.
> *IT Support Specialist | Help Desk → Junior SysAdmin | Active Directory · Microsoft 365 ·
> PowerShell | Building a public IT-ops portfolio*

## About section (skeleton)
1. **Who you are + target role** (1–2 lines).
2. **What you can do** — the operational skills (work a queue, AD/M365 admin, troubleshoot
   methodically, document).
3. **Proof** — "I build my skills in public: [link to repo]. Every project ships a runbook,
   troubleshooting guide, KB article, and incident report."
4. **The honest framing** — "hands-on lab/personal projects" — never implied production experience.

## Featured section
Pin 2–3 of your flagship artifacts (link directly to the files):
- The onboarding/offboarding runbook + script.
- A troubleshooting guide with a real diagnostic spine.
- A capstone metrics report.

## Posting (the build-in-public engine)
Post a short update at each milestone — this is what actually surfaces you to recruiters.
**Post template:**
> *Milestone: finished the Identity & Directory module of my IT-ops portfolio.*
> *What I built: a scripted onboarding process (PowerShell + AD + M365) and a GPO troubleshooting
> guide.*
> *What I learned: how Group Policy actually processes (LSDOU) and why `gpresult /r` is the first
> thing you check.*
> *It's all public: [link]. #ITSupport #HelpDesk #PowerShell #ActiveDirectory*

Cadence: one post per completed module (every few lessons), not daily noise.

## Certifications
List them as you pass (A+, Network+, MS-900, MD-102, ITIL 4). Note "in progress" honestly. The
cert + the portfolio together beat either alone.

## Engagement
- Follow IT-support / sysadmin creators and comment substantively (not "great post!").
- Connect with recruiters in your target market with a one-line note referencing your portfolio.
- Join IT-support communities; answer questions using what you've learned — visible competence.

## Do / Don't
- ✅ Link to specific artifacts; ✅ keep keywords true; ✅ label lab work as lab work.
- ❌ Don't inflate to "Senior"; ❌ don't claim employer projects; ❌ don't post real data.
