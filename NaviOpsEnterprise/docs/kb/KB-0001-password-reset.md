# Reset your password

**Applies to:** all staff with a `corp.example` account · **KB ID:** KB-0001
**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-21 · **Owner:** Service Desk

## What this covers
How to reset your own password using self-service, and what to do if you can't.

## Before you start
- You'll need access to your registered **MFA method** (authenticator app, phone, or security
  questions). If you haven't enrolled, see KB-0010 or contact the Service Desk.

## Reset it yourself (fastest)
1. Go to **https://passwordreset.corp.example** (or click **"Can't access your account?"** on the
   sign-in page).
2. Enter your work email (`firstname.lastname@corp.example`).
3. Complete the **identity check** (approve the app prompt / enter the code sent to your phone).
4. Enter a **new password** that meets the rules below, twice.
5. You'll see "Your password has been reset." Sign in with the new password everywhere.

## Password rules
- At least 12 characters, with upper + lower case, a number, and a symbol.
- Not one of your recent passwords; not your name or "Password123".
- Tip: use a **passphrase** — three random words + a number/symbol (easy to remember, hard to guess).

## After you reset
Update the password on **every device** that signs in as you — especially your **phone's mail
app** and any **mapped drives** — or you may get **locked out** from the old saved password (see
KB-0002).

## Still not working?
- The reset page doesn't recognize you, or you have no MFA method enrolled → contact the Service
  Desk; they will verify your identity before resetting it for you.
- Provide: your name, your work email, and what step failed. Reference **KB-0001**.
